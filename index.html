Why Kenya?

Kenya, home of the Big Five

If you're dreaming of exciting adventures and unforgettable wildlife encounters, Kenya should be at the top of your safari bucket list! This East African gem is renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and its incredible wildlife. Kenya is home to the iconic 'Big Five': lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino. These legendary animals were once the most challenging to hunt on foot. Today they are the crown jewels of safari sightings. Kenya is considered one of the best places on Earth to see them in their natural habitat.

With over 50 national parks and reserves, Kenya offers a wealth of safari experiences. From the vast savannahs of the Maasai Mara, where the Great Migration comes alive, to the rugged landscapes of Tsavo and the stunning vistas of Amboseli with its views of Mount Kilimanjaro, each location has its own unique charm. Whether you're tracking big cats on game drives, observing elephants at watering holes or spotting rare birds in secluded wetlands, Kenya promises endless excitement for every nature enthusiast. So grab your binoculars, charge your camera and get ready; in Kenya, adventure is always just around the corner!

My favorite places in Kenya

Nairobi

Nairobi is the bustling capital of Kenya, where urban energy meets with stunning natural beauty. You can spot giraffes and rhinos without ever leaving the city! It is the only capital in the world that has a national park right on its doorstep.

What I like about it

What makes Nairobi a must-visit is its unique blend of city life and safari experiences. You can feed endangered Rothschild giraffes at the Giraffe Centre in the morning, explore local art and food scenes in the afternoon, and end your day with sundowners overlooking wild plains. It's the perfect jumpstart to any Kenyan adventure! It's the ideal way to kick off any Kenyan adventure!

East Tsavo

Tsavo East, one of Kenya’s oldest and largest national parks, features vast red-dust plains adorned with baobab trees. It is renowned for its “red elephants,” which bathe in the iron-rich soil, giving them a distinct coppery glow.

What I like about it

If you're craving raw, untamed wilderness, Tsavo national park is your place. Wildlife viewing feels like a true expedition here, thanks to fewer crowds and plenty of space. you'll find yourself immersed in the landscape and the excitement of discovery. Whether it's lions lazing near the Galana River or herds of elephants kicking up dust at dusk, Tsavo delivers truly offers safari magic.

Diani

Diani Beach is a tropical paradise on Kenya's southern coast, famous for its powdery white sands, swaying palm trees and turquoise waters. It's the perfect coastal escape after your safari adventure.

What I like about it

Diani is more than just a beach; it's an experience. In one day, you can snorkel in coral reefs, kite-surf over clear lagoons or take a dhow cruise at sunset. Whether you're relaxing at a luxury resort or sipping coconut water with your toes in the sand, Diani wraps you in a state of pure bliss.
